Tips for Passing your Splunk Certification Demo

Demo Overview:

Your demo should be approximately 30 minutes and should tell a compelling story about Splunk as a solution to a business problem. Reading the script will not constitute a pass. Use the script as a study guide. You won't have time to hit EVERY point covered in the script. It is more important to have a good story to tell.

At the start of the demo you will set the stage by telling the scorer the scenario and customer example that you wish to use.

Demo Scoring Categories:

You will be scored on the following categories: 

1.     Setting the Stage:

a.      Be sure to role-play as if you are presenting to a real customer; ask questions, engage the audience, understand the customer's business pains. (5 points)

                                               i.     Come to the demo with key pains from a previous discovery session

                                              ii.     Validate what was learned from the previous discovery session

                                            iii.     Continue to discover more about the customer throughout the demo

                                            iv.     Relate the demo scenario to the customer example

b.     Introduce the concept of Splunk as a platform for machine data. (5 points)

                                               i.     Define machine data including the pains and complexity

                                              ii.     Provide a summary introduction of how Splunk solves that problem and continue to illustrate throughout the demo i.e. Splunk mission statement

                                            iii.     Create a sense of urgency to look at machine data to solve business problems

2.     Splunk Differentiators: (5 points)

a.      These are carefully crafted to help you sell the value of Splunk; be sure to include as many as possible during your demo as they fit into the story.

3.     Using the Splunk Interface (5 points)

a.      Illustrate various features of the interface

b.     Tell a convincing story that the UI is easy to use

4.     More than a Search Engine (5 points)

a.      Illustrate that Splunk is more than a search tool by talking about correlation, data augmentation, alerts, the value of visualizations or other relevant features.

5.     Value Selling VS. a Dashboard Tour (5 points)

a.      Show the dashboards in the context of solving business problems

b.     Do NOT show and describe a series of dashboards out of context

6.     Use & Proof (5 points)

a.      Integrate customer examples throughout the demo for validation

7.     Presentation Skills (5 points)

a.      Be prepared and fluent, do not memorize your story

b.     Be confident and establish credibility

c.      Keep a good pace and with smooth transition between topics

d.     Be careful to minimize mouse movements

e.      Be attentive to your audience
